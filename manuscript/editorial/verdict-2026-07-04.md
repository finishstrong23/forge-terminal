## Genuine strengths (protect these)

- The opening ("stay inside" / "Drev had my money") — danger, humor, class, voice.
- The Hollow's coherent symbolic vocabulary: silver forest, living Hall,
  shadow-fox, marks, binding, Veil Nights, thirteen territories. The fox is a
  mascot-grade asset.
- Restrained romance told through behavior (chairs, tea, lingering cold) —
  over-reiterated, not underwritten.
- A real series engine: sealed wing, final knocking, figure with Aleksei's eyes.
- Book One reaches a milestone (Claim chosen, recognition won, kiss), not a
  pure cliffhanger.

## The ten problems

1. **Too slow to reveal the story.** Old Claim / dying-Hollow explanation lands
   too late; the convening occupies only the last ~5%. Target 95–105k words
   (cut 20–30k). Key reveal by 25–35%; active opposition before the midpoint.
2. **Chapter Two replays Chapter One.** Keep Aleksei's Hunt-sense and the
   abnormal binding; cut the repeated alley business by ~half.
3. **Formulaic prose machinery.** "specific quality of", "not X, not Y, but",
   "I filed this", "looked at me for a long moment", observation followed by
   two abstract re-interpretations. Fix by deletion, not synonyms.
4. **Kira and Aleksei sound identical.** Both narrate in register/mechanism/
   framework/cataloguing language. Kira should be concrete, tactical, street,
   money. Aleksei should be older, stranger, less managerial.
5. **Kira is right too often.** She needs at least one major, public,
   consequential error of her own before the final speech.
6. **Aleksei is too ideal.** He needs one consequential choice the reader
   cannot immediately excuse, which damages trust and forces opposition.
7. **Captivity vs. tenderness unreconciled.** The book must confront the
   consent objection explicitly (open door she returns through, symmetrical
   duty on him, renounced entitlement, independent advocate, or rewriting the
   law that misnamed covenant as ownership).
8. **The world feels smaller than the premise.** Mortal world vanishes; the
   thirteen territories are votes, not places. **Rename Cael or Kael.**
9. **Climax is too easy.** Votes counted in advance, allies vote as expected.
   Needs a defection, a cost, uncomfortable evidence from Zora, a conditional
   recognition, or a win that unleashes something unauthorized.
10. **Title/market mismatch + collision.** *The Hollow Hunt* promises action;
    the book is atmospheric slow-burn gothic romantasy. Existing series *The
    Hollow's Hunt* (Selena Winters) creates search/brand confusion. Rebrand
    around a proprietary term (Nochval, Old Claim, Veil) after a title search.
    KDP locks title metadata after publication.

## Recommended revision passes

- **Pass 1 — structure:** one-page beat sheet of only load-bearing events;
  restructure to: 0–25% hunt/binding/entry/first mark/early dying-evidence;
  25–50% Claim reveal, active opposition, Kira's consequential mistake;
  50–75% restoration causes damage, Aleksei's trust-breaking choice, Kira's
  independent standing; 75–90% earned repair, a failed alliance, known cost;
  90–100% convening with reversal, sacrifice, imperfect victory.
- **Pass 2 — cut and combine:** Ch2 −40–60%; merge early Hall exploration;
  stop re-narrating established behaviors; compress mark→explain→process
  loops; halve scene breaks; cut repeated vote arithmetic; prune "binding"
  and the tic list to the strongest uses.
- **Pass 3 — character cost:** Kira's unarticulable failure; Aleksei's
  inexcusable decision; Zora/Maren given uncomfortable truth; Sable/Vasilisa/
  Radovan given non-aligned desires.
- **Pass 4 — voice differentiation + full copyedit** with a continuity bible.

## Positioning

> Adult gothic romantic fantasy; ultra-slow-burn, forced proximity, immortal
> guardian, sentient house, ancient political magic, competent streetwise
> heroine.

> On the one night no one may leave home, a streetwise thief is claimed by an
> immortal hunter — and discovers that her presence may be the dying realm's
> last chance to survive.

Not spicy romantasy; do not market it as one. Cover: haunted beauty (silver
forest, fox, Hall, Veil-lit city), not chase-thriller or shirtless-immortal.

## Production notes (post-text-lock)

- v1 file is a submission manuscript, not a book interior. Separate print
  (likely 6×9, mirrored margins, running heads, page numbers, unindented
  openers, embedded fonts, print PDF) and ebook (working nav TOC) builds.
- Order a physical proof; inspect both previewers.
- Own ISBNs / imprint for a long-term brand; decide KDP Select deliberately.
- Register US copyright on the finished book; keep dated versions and a
  chain-of-title folder; written contracts for all commissioned art.
- Build the canon bible before drafting deep into Book Two; 13 territories
  each need biome/culture/conflict/symbol, not names at a council.
- Property fits serialized TV/animation better than film; adaptation
  obstacle is interiority — externalize marks, binding, the dying, politics.

## Publication sequence

1. Developmental rewrite (−20–30k words).
2. 8–12 genre-native beta readers; ask where they got impatient, when they
   understood the plot, whether Kira's choice felt free, whether the climax
   felt earned.
3. Revise again. 4. Copyedit + continuity pass. 5. Lock title/brand.
6. Genre-accurate cover. 7. Format print/ebook separately.
8. Verify `rmrime.com/hollow` delivers the bonus before ARCs.
9. Physical proof. 10. Release only with a stable Book Two draft.
